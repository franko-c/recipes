Wild Mushroom Risotto

risotto is the ultimate comfort food, and adding earthy, wild mushrooms elevates it to a luxurious meal. this dish is perfect for dinner parties or cozy nights in.

mastering a risotto at home comes down to three things:

  1. the right rice! always use arborio rice for that creamy texture.
  2. constant stirring - this technique releases the starches and makes risotto beautifully creamy.
  3. layering flavors - wild mushrooms, good broth, and Parmesan cheese make all the difference.

the trick?

add broth gradually. don’t rush it! slow cooking and absorption are key to perfect risotto.

ingredients

for the base:

  • 1 cup arborio rice
  • 4 cups vegetable or chicken broth (warm)
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced

for the mushrooms:

  • 2 cups wild mushrooms (e.g., cremini, shiitake, or chanterelles)
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • 1/4 cup dry white wine

optional garnishes:

  • 1/4 cup Parmesan cheese, grated
  • fresh parsley, chopped
  • truffle oil (optional, for extra decadence)

instructions

  1. prepare the mushrooms

    2. heat butter in a skillet. Sauté mushrooms until golden brown. Deglaze with white wine, let it evaporate, and set mushrooms aside.

  2. cook the aromatics

    3. in a large pan, heat olive oil. Sauté onions and garlic until soft and fragrant.

  3. toast the rice

    4. Add arborio rice to the pan and toast for 1–2 minutes, stirring constantly.

  4. add broth gradually

    5. add broth one ladle at a time, stirring frequently. Wait until most liquid is absorbed before adding more.

  5. combine and finish

    6. stir in the cooked mushrooms and Parmesan cheese. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

  6. serve and garnish

    7. plate the risotto and garnish with parsley and a drizzle of truffle oil, if desired.

notes and tips

    broth temperature

  • Keep the broth warm throughout the cooking process—it helps maintain the dish's temperature and consistency.

    • don’t rush

  • Risotto is a dish of patience. Take your time and stir frequently for the best results.

    • experiment

  • try adding other ingredients like peas, asparagus, or even shrimp for variety.

serving guide

serve immediately while hot and creamy. pair with a crisp white wine like Pinot Grigio or Sauvignon Blanc for a restaurant-quality meal at home.

gather around the table, savor each bite, and enjoy this culinary classic!
