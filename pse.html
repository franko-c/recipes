Pad See Ew

Pad See Ew - which means “stir fried soy sauce noodles” - is an extremely popular Thai street food meal and one of the most popular noodles dishes at Thai restaurants.

making a great Pad See Ew at home simply comes down to two things:

  1. the right sauce! Basic recipes online will instruct you to use little more than just soy sauce and sugar. It takes a little more than that!
  2. caramelising the noodles - getting a little caramelisation on the noodles makes all the difference between an “ok” and “wow, it’s JUST like you get at restaurants!”.

the trick?

remove the stir fry ingredients. Cook the noodles with sauce separately. Less stuff in the wok (or skillet) = easier to caramelise the noodles.

if you’ve got a giant restaurant wok burner, you don’t need to do the noodles separately!

ingredients

for the sauce:

  • 2 tbsp dark soy sauce (for that rich, caramelized depth)
  • 1 tbsp light soy sauce (adds saltiness and balance)

for the dish:

  • 8 oz wide rice noodles (fresh or pre-soaked if dried)
  • 1 cup protein (chicken, pork, tofu, or shrimp—whatever suits your mood)
  • 1 cup Chinese broccoli or bok choy (the crisp stems are a secret weapon for texture)
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp oil (vegetable or peanut oil works best for high heat)

optional garnishes:

  • chilli flakes
  • vinegar
  • lime wedges

instructions

  1. prepare the sauce

    2. in a small bowl, mix the dark soy sauce and light soy sauce. this two-soy combo is where the magic begins.

  2. cook the protein

    3. heat a wok or large skillet over high heat until it’s almost smoking. Add oil and sear your protein until golden.

    “High heat is key,” Thai street vendors say, “to give your dish that coveted smoky ‘wok hei’ flavor.”

  3. add garlic

    4. toss in the minced garlic and stir-fry for 10–15 seconds. Don’t let it burn—just enough to release that irresistible aroma.

  4. stir-fry noodles

    5. push the protein aside and add the rice noodles. Pour in the sauce and toss vigorously. Use tongs or a spatula to ensure every noodle gets coated.

  5. vegetables

    6. add the Chinese broccoli or bok choy, stems first. Cook until tender but still crunchy, about 2–3 minutes

  6. serve and garnish

    7. plate it up hot, and if you’re feeling bold, sprinkle chili flakes or a splash of vinegar for some zing. Serve with lime wedges for brightness.

notes and tips

    wok tip

  • a seasoned wok is like an artist’s brush — every dish you cook enhances its flavor. don’t have one? use a heavy-bottomed skillet and crank up the heat!

    • noodle handling

  • fresh rice noodles are delicate, so handle them gently. if you’re using dried ones, soak them in warm water until soft but not mushy.

    • flavor balance

  • the sauce is the soul of Pad See Ew. adjust the soy sauces to find the balance that suits your taste.

serving guide

serve with a chilled Thai iced tea or a crisp cucumber salad to cool the palate.

This dish is as much about the experience as it is about the flavor—gather your loved ones, grab some chopsticks, and enjoy it while it’s fresh off the pan.
