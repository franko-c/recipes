makaronia tou fournou

makaronia tou fournou, or “pasta of the oven,” is cyprus’s take on pastitsio. with a rich, cinnamon-scented meat sauce, pasta layers, and creamy béchamel, it’s a dish that feels like a warm hug.

getting this dish just right comes down to:

  1. seasoning - cinnamon and nutmeg make the sauce unforgettable.
  2. layering - proper layering gives it the distinct texture and look.
  3. a creamy béchamel - velvety smooth and slightly golden from the oven.

the secret?

patience during assembly ensures perfect layers every time.

ingredients

for the meat sauce:

  • 500g ground beef or pork
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup tomato paste
  • 1/2 cup red wine (optional)
  • 1 tsp cinnamon
  • 1/2 tsp nutmeg
  • salt and pepper to taste

for the pasta:

  • 500g bucatini or macaroni
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1/4 cup grated halloumi or parmesan

for the béchamel:

  • 4 tbsp butter
  • 4 tbsp flour
  • 3 cups milk
  • 1/4 tsp nutmeg
  • salt to taste
  • 2 egg yolks (optional, for richness)

optional garnish:

  • grated cheese
  • fresh parsley

instructions

  1. make the meat sauce

    2. sauté onions and garlic in olive oil until soft. add ground meat and cook until browned. stir in tomato paste, wine, cinnamon, nutmeg, salt, and pepper. simmer until thickened.

  2. cook the pasta

    3. boil pasta until al dente, drain, and toss with olive oil and a bit of grated cheese. set aside.

  3. prepare the béchamel

    4. melt butter in a pan, whisk in flour, and cook for 1 minute. slowly add milk, whisking constantly until smooth. season with nutmeg and salt. remove from heat and mix in egg yolks if using.

  4. assemble the dish

    5. in a baking dish, layer half the pasta, all the meat sauce, the remaining pasta, and top with béchamel. spread evenly.

  5. bake

    6. bake at 180°c (350°f) for 40–50 minutes, until the top is golden and bubbling.

  6. serve

    7. let it cool for 10 minutes before slicing. garnish with grated cheese or parsley if desired.

notes and tips

    cheese swap

  • halloumi adds a cypriot touch, but parmesan or romano work just as well.

    • pasta choice

  • bucatini is traditional, but ziti or rigatoni can be used in a pinch.

    • leftovers

  • this dish reheats beautifully. just pop it in the oven for 15 minutes at 160°c (320°f).

serving guide

serve with a crisp green salad and a glass of red wine. it’s a hearty meal perfect for sharing.

enjoy this taste of cyprus with loved ones, and let the flavors transport you to a mediterranean feast!
